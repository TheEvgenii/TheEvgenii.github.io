Findings overview

The memory footprint of 24/7 recording in places where there isn't a lot of movement can make it problematic to find a specific moment when viewing video data. Therefore, using the correct method and protocol to deal with various situations with video surveillance cameras can facilitate and improve the surveillance work significantly, as well as reduce the recording of unnecessary material.

Cameras opposite each other

Left Camera

Detected movement, which helped to start filming two cameras, as well as send a notification to the proctor


Right Camera

Start recording after receiving info from the left camera about the detected object.

All this is possible due to the fact that we have marked the field for which the cameras should follow. As soon as there is movement in this field, the technology stores the data and waits for it to exceed the norm. Then the task of capturing and sending notifications will follow.

Cameras at an angle of 90 degrees

The right camera is sideways in this situation, as it is directed towards the entrance to the apartment. The recording will not occur until the cameras receive data about a moving object in the frame. If a moving object disappears from the field of view of two cameras, then the countdown starts from 1-30 seconds. Both cameras will then enter non-recording mode.
